# Ideal Client Profile (ICP) Strategy

This document outlines the Ideal Client Profile (ICP) and targeting strategy based on your unique background and skills.

---

## 1. Synthesis of Strengths

*   **Primary Goal:** To find a remote gig job with the highest probability of success by identifying the best-fit roles and companies.
*   **Your "Unfair Advantage":** 15 years of deep, hands-on experience in the hospitality industry. This provides you with invaluable domain expertise.
*   **Your Cultural Fluency:** A background that allows you to understand and communicate effectively within different cultural and ideological circles (specifically, conservative and "classical liberal").

---

## 2. The Proposed Direction: The Specialist's Approach

We will adopt a highly-focused, two-pronged strategy. Instead of a broad search, we will target niches where your unique background makes you an exceptionally valuable candidate.

### Primary Target (ICP #1): Hospitality Technology

This is our **A-List**, and we will focus 70% of our effort here. You are not just "a developer" to these companies; you are a potential user, a subject matter expert, and a developer all in one.

*   **Why it's the highest likelihood of success:** Your outreach is not a cold call; it's a message from a peer. Your one-sentence pitch becomes incredibly powerful: **"As a developer with 15 years of experience as a chef and kitchen manager, I understand the problems you are solving for restaurants better than almost any other developer you could hire."**
*   **Companies to Target:** We will build a list of companies in these sub-industries:
    *   **Restaurant POS & Management:** Toast, Square for Restaurants, Lightspeed, TouchBistro.
    *   **Inventory & Supply Chain:** PlateIQ, Craftable, and the technology arms of distributors like Sysco or US Foods.
    *   **Reservation & Guest Management:** Resy, OpenTable, Tock.
    *   **Labor & Scheduling Software:** 7shifts, HotSchedules.

### Secondary Target (ICP #2): Conservative & "Classical Liberal" Organizations

This is our **B-List**, and we will focus 30% of our effort here. This is a cultural play where your background and communication style can create a strong sense of alignment and trust.

*   **Why it's an advantage:** In a world where cultural fit is a major hiring factor, your ability to navigate these circles is a unique asset. You can understand the company's mission and values on a deeper level.
*   **How to Target:** This is less about finding companies via their tech stack and more about finding them via their identity. We will search for:
    *   **Mission-Driven Companies:** Businesses that are explicitly "pro-free market," "pro-liberty," etc.
    *   **Specialized Job Boards:** Such as those for alumni of Hillsdale or other conservative institutions.
    *   **Think Tanks & Media:** Organizations like The Heritage Foundation, Cato Institute, or The Daily Wire, which have their own internal development teams.

---

## 3. The Direction Going Forward

1.  We will **prioritize the Hospitality Tech ICP**. This is your strongest play and offers the highest probability of generating leads for high-value gig work.
2.  We will simultaneously build a smaller, secondary list of organizations that align with your cultural fluency.
3.  We will tailor your outreach for each ICP. The message to a restaurant tech company will be very different from the message to a conservative think tank, but both will leverage your unique background.
