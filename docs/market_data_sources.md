# Job Market Data Sources

This document outlines different sources for both quantitative (statistical) and qualitative (anecdotal) job market data. A complete picture requires combining both types of sources.

---

## 1. Specific Quantitative Data (Beyond BLS)

To get more granular than the BLS, we can look for data that is more real-time, role-specific, and location-specific.

*   **Salary Aggregators:** These sites provide more up-to-date, role-specific salary data based on user submissions.
    *   **Levels.fyi:** The best resource for compensation data, primarily for mid-to-large tech companies. It's excellent for understanding salary bands and total compensation (stock, bonus).
    *   **Glassdoor & PayScale:** These have broader data sets across more company sizes and locations. They are good for getting a general salary baseline for a specific role in a specific city.

*   **Live Job Board Data:** The job boards themselves can be analyzed as a real-time indicator of demand.
    *   **LinkedIn/Indeed Search:** The number of open positions for specific keywords (e.g., "Laravel Developer in Kalamazoo, MI") is a direct, real-time measure of local demand.
    *   **Stack Overflow Developer Survey:** An annual survey that provides a wealth of information on what technologies are most used, most loved, and pay the most, often with regional breakdowns.

---

## 2. Qualitative "Anecdotal" Data

This is where to find the "word on the street" to understand the *sentiment* and *hiring practices* that statistics don't show.

*   **Online Communities (The Best Source):**
    *   **Reddit:** The primary source for anecdotal data. Subreddits like `r/cscareerquestions`, `r/webdev`, and language-specific subs like `r/PHP` or `r/devops` are filled with thousands of posts about job searches, interview experiences, and market conditions.
    *   **Hacker News (news.ycombinator.com):** Known for high-quality, candid discussions about the tech industry. Their monthly "Who is Hiring?" and "Who Wants to be Hired?" threads are a treasure trove of information.
    *   **Blind:** An anonymous professional network where employees from various tech companies candidly discuss salaries, interviews, and company culture.

*   **Local Meetups & User Groups:** Searching for local "PHP User Groups" or "DevOps Meetups" can provide direct experience from the local market.

---

## 3. Free and Reliable Data Sources

This section provides a breakdown of the sources from the list above that are both free and generally considered reliable for their specific purpose.

### Quantitative Sources (for numbers, trends)

These sources are best for getting objective data.

*   **Levels.fyi:**
    *   **Free:** Yes.
    *   **Reliable:** Yes, it is considered the gold standard for compensation data in mid-to-large tech companies.

*   **Stack Overflow Developer Survey:**
    *   **Free:** Yes.
    *   **Reliable:** Yes, this is one of the most comprehensive and respected annual reports in the industry, excellent for understanding technology and salary trends.

*   **Live Job Board Analysis (LinkedIn, Indeed):**
    *   **Free:** Yes.
    *   **Reliable:** Yes, for measuring real-time *demand*. The number of job postings for a specific role is a direct indicator of the current hiring landscape.

*   **Glassdoor & PayScale:**
    *   **Free:** Yes.
    *   **Reliable:** Moderately. Useful for getting a general salary ballpark, but can be less precise than other sources.

### Qualitative Sources (for sentiment, anecdotes)

These sources are not statistically reliable for numbers, but they are **extremely reliable** for understanding the *human side* of the job market.

*   **Reddit** (e.g., `r/cscareerquestions`, `r/webdev`):
    *   **Free:** Yes.
    *   **Reliable:** Yes, for understanding current market *sentiment*, common interview questions, and real-world job search experiences.

*   **Hacker News:**
    *   **Free:** Yes.
    *   **Reliable:** Yes, for gauging the opinions of a highly experienced segment of the tech community and for discovering who is hiring via the monthly "Who is Hiring?" threads.

*   **Blind:**
    *   **Free:** Yes.
    *   **Reliable:** Yes, for getting unfiltered, anonymous insights into the interview processes and compensation packages at specific tech companies.
