## 3. Job Analysis

This section provides a deeper analysis of the role's true nature, industry-standard titles, leadership level, and compensation.

### Role & Title Analysis
The title "Software Administrative Support" is very literal. Based on the responsibilities—which include CRM optimization, process improvement, data reporting, and technical support—this is a classic hybrid role common in small-to-medium-sized businesses. It blends IT support with business operations.

More common industry titles for this set of responsibilities include:
*   **Sales Operations Specialist:** Focuses on the sales process, CRM, and reporting.
*   **Marketing Operations Specialist:** Focuses on the marketing tech stack and campaign support.
*   **Business Systems Analyst:** Focuses on improving business processes through technology.
*   **Digital Operations Coordinator:** A generalist title for managing internal digital tools.

The company may be using "Administrative Support" to align with a specific internal job-leveling or pay scale. However, the duties described are more aligned with an "Operations Specialist" or "Analyst" role.

### Leadership Level
This is an **Individual Contributor** role, not a management position. 
*   **Evidence:** The role involves *supporting* team members, *assisting* with communications, and *preparing reports for* leadership. There is no mention of direct reports or team management responsibilities. 
*   **Implication:** This means you will be evaluated on your direct skills and ability to execute tasks and improve systems, not on your ability to manage people.

### Pay & Benefits Analysis
*   **Stated Pay Range:** $20.00 - $27.50 per hour.
*   **Annualized Range:** Approximately **$41,600 - $57,200** per year.
*   **Market Comparison (Portage/Kalamazoo, MI Area):** Research for a comparable title like "Sales Operations Specialist" in Michigan shows an average salary of around $90,000/year, with the low end (10th percentile) being approximately $75,000/year.
*   **Conclusion:** The provided pay scale is **significantly below the market average** for a role with these operational and technical responsibilities. This is a critical point to be aware of. The "Software Administrative Support" title may be used to justify this pay band.
*   **Benefits:** The posting only lists "Paid time off" and "Flexible schedule." The absence of any mention of health insurance, retirement plans (like a 401k), or other standard benefits is a major point of inquiry. You should be prepared to ask for details about the full benefits package during the interview.
